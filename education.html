Enkhuizen Nautical College

The campus is located close to the city center and just a 5-minute walk from the train station. Once you arrive, you will notice “Kaatje,” their unique school ship used for nautical teaching. The school features a gymnasium, 4 classrooms, a PC room, a library, and exclusive parking spaces. All rooms are centrally heated, air-purified, and have internet connections, PCs, and projectors. The auditorium has a capacity of 100 and is perfect for meetings and presentations.

Courses

Wind Assisted Ship Propulsion (WASP) Course

This course explores recent developments in sailing commercial freight, focusing on the importance of this green technology in the marine energy transition. It covers many wind-assist devices, such as kites, Fletter rotors, segmented wings, soft sails, and Cousteau sails. The course also covers the history and future of WASP technologies, fluid physics, and mathematics.

Kleine Zeilvaart (KZV) Course

This course prepares you to become captain of commercial sailing boats under 500 tonnes and (chief) officers of sailing vessels under 500 tonnes. Among the subjects covered are navigation, nautical instruments, weather, practical navigation, construction, ship stability, maneuvering, collision laws, maritime engineering, medical assistance at sea, maritime English, and sailing theory. Although it's not required, having sailing experience is helpful.

Grote Zeilvaart (GZV) Course

This course prepares students to be captains and chief mates of up to 3000-ton sailing boats with no sailing area restrictions. It covers celestial navigation, meteorology, oceanography, advanced ship maneuvering, trip planning, and other topics. Along with Radar Navigation Operational Level (RNOL) certification, it involves hands-on training on the "Kaatje." Students must have a KZV diploma or its equivalent to be eligible.
