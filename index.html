Wind Assisted Ship Propulsion is the route forward

WASP is an effective short- to medium-term mitigation option for the maritime industry’s essential course towards rapid decarbonisation. The global shipping industry carries approximately 90% of world trade by weight and accounts for 3% of carbon dioxide emissions. Continuing with business as usual, greenhouse gas emissions are expected to increase by 50 – 250% by 2050. To address this, the International Maritime Organisation has introduced requirements aiming to reduce total yearly emissions by at least 50% of 2008 levels by 2050 but full decarbonisation of international shipping by around 2050 may be necessary to stay within the limits of the Paris Climate Agreement. In the short- to medium-term harnessing the wind offers the best route to reducing emissions as research into alternative fuel sources continues.
